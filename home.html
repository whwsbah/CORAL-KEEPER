Although coral reefs in more than 100 countries cover only 0.2% of the ocean floor, they support the safety, coastal protection, well-being, food and economic security of hundreds of millions of people.

The continuously increasing temperature is one of the most severe contributors of the coral bleaching.

And then,coral keepers comb though the Coral-algal symbiosis systems in detail,digging into a unique way to construct a new branch of algae,named zooxanthellae-Hsp26,with the technology of synthetic biology.

Introduce Hsp26 gene into zooxanthellae. Transgenic yellow worm algae and control group were treated with high temperature stress.
By comparing the survival amount of the two, we can determine whether the transgenic flavidium algae can obtain higher heat resistance.


The improvement of heat tolerance of zooxanthellae is helpful for the symbiotic system of zooxanthellae and coral to survive longer in hot summer, and the photosynthesis and transcription expression can be improved, thus reducing the influence of high temperature on zooxanthellae internally.

Heat resistant treatment of zooxanthella can help corals better adapt to the environment, which can improve the survival rate of corals in the current environment of rising ocean temperatures caused by the greenhouse effect, so that they can better survive in the ocean.


This in turn provides habitat and the basis of the food chain for many Marine organisms, and is critical to maintaining the stability and balance of the entire Marine ecosystem.


At the same time, the improvement of the survival rate of coral can improve the efficiency of filtering various particles in seawater, improve the rate of carbon dioxide conversion into calcium carbonate, and help to reduce the carbon dioxide content in the atmosphere and reduce the greenhouse effect.
